How to track my Shein package?

Ordertracker provides a simple solution to track your Shein packages. To begin, you'll need a Shein tracking number provided by the sender or the retailer.

Enter your tracking number in the above field and hit "enter." Our tracker will then fetch the latest tracking details for your package.

You'll see a timeline showing your package's journey, including its current location, transit history, and expected delivery date. This keeps you informed about your Shein package in real time.

Where can I find my Shein tracking number?

Finding your Shein tracking number is usually simple. When you buy an item online or send a package, the tracking number is typically given by the online store or sender. If you're the recipient, the sender often provides it via email, text, or on the order confirmation page.

For online purchases, log in to your store account to find the tracking number in your order history or tracking section. You can also find it in shipping notifications sent to your email or phone.

Once you have the tracking number, you can easily use Ordertracker to monitor your package's progress. This tool shows your package's journey and estimated delivery date.

Why isn't my Shein package moving in the package tracking history?

Dealing with a Shein package that appears stuck in tracking can be frustrating. Yet, there are steps to tackle this. If you've been using Ordertracker and see no progress, start by being patient. Delays, like customs or logistical issues, can arise.

If the delay remains, contact Shein or the sender for specifics. They can offer insight or investigate. Keep your tracking number ready for a smoother process. Sometimes, misrouting or address errors cause delays. Confirm the details provided to you by the sender. Remember, Shein customer service or the sender can help resolve shipping delays.

When I track my Shein package, why does it show as "returned"?

If you're tracking your Shein package and it's marked as "returned", this usually means that the package has been sent back to the sender or the shipment origin location for a specific reason. There are a few common explanations for why a Shein package might be labeled as "returned":

Incorrect address

An unclear or inaccurate address from sender or recipient might lead Shein to be unable to deliver, resulting in a return.

Unsuccessful delivery attempts

If multiple delivery attempts fail, Shein may return the package instead of holding it indefinitely.

Unclaimed package

If the recipient doesn't collect the package within a timeframe from a post office or delivery center, Shein might return it.

Customs issues

International packages can be return due to customs problems like missing or incorrect documents.

If your Shein package is "returned" in tracking, contact the sender or Shein customer service. This clarifies the return reason and lets you explore solutions like resending or refunding. Please note that return procedures can vary based on Shein policies and circumstances.

Why does the Shein parcel tracking timeline indicate that my order cannot be found?

When your Shein parcel tracking lacks information, it may indicate a "cannot be found" message. This happens for a few reasons:

Incorrect tracking number

Ensure your tracking number is accurate. Even a small mistake can prevent the system from recognizing your package.

Delayed update

Sometimes, tracking details might be slower to update in Shein system. Wait and check again later for potential updates.

Recently shipped

If the package was recently sent, it might not be processed by Shein yet. Tracking should appear shortly.

How long does it take for Shein to deliver a shipment?

The shipping time and cost may vary depending on factors such as the shipping destination, shipping method chosen, and any applicable promotions.   • Standard Shipping: Shein provides standard shipping to numerous countries around the world. The estimated delivery time for standard shipping typically ranges from 7 to 15 business days. However, this may vary based on the specific location and customs processing.
  • Express Shipping: For those who want their orders to arrive more quickly, Shein offers express shipping options. The estimated delivery time for express shipping usually ranges from 3 to 7 business days. This expedited shipping option is often preferred by customers who are looking for a faster delivery.
  • Free Shipping: In some cases, Shein provides free standard shipping on orders above a certain threshold. This offer varies depending on the region, promotion, and time of purchase. Free shipping offers are generally an attractive feature for customers, as they can save on shipping costs.

During the checkout process, customers can review the shipping charges specific to their order. To ensure reliable and timely delivery, Shein collaborates with various shipping partners globally. The choice of shipping partner may again depend on factors such as the shipping destination and the available services in that region. Shein strives to work with reputable shipping partners who can efficiently handle the transportation and delivery of packages.

Apart from shipping time, it is important to consider the order processing time. After placing an order on the Shein website or mobile app, there is generally a processing period before the package is shipped. This processing time typically varies depending on the availability of the item, the complexity of the order, and the volume of orders being processed at that particular time.

It is important to note that the aforementioned delivery times and shipping costs are subject to change, depending on various factors. Shein strives to provide accurate information to customers during the checkout process. What shipping carriers does Shein work with?

Shein primarily works with a variety of shipping carriers in order to deliver their products to customers worldwide. The specific shipping carrier used depends on the destination country and the shipping method chosen by the customer during checkout. Some popular shipping carriers that Shein often collaborates with include:

  • Postal Services: Shein frequently partners with local postal services to ensure efficient delivery. In the United States, they collaborate with USPS (United States Postal Service), while in the United Kingdom, they work with Royal Mail.
  • Courier Services: Shein utilizes renowned courier services such as DHL (Dalsey, Hillblom, and Lynn), UPS (United Parcel Service), and FedEx (Federal Express) for international shipments. These courier services enable faster and more reliable delivery for customers.
  • Local Partners: In certain countries, Shein has established partnerships with local logistics providers to ensure smooth shipping operations. These local partners have in-depth knowledge of the region's infrastructure, ensuring efficient delivery to customers.

Tracking numbers from Shein typically consist of a combination of letters and numbers. The format and length of the tracking number can vary depending on the shipping carrier used. For example, if DHL is the carrier, a tracking number from Shein might look like "1234567890". Similarly, a tracking number from USPS could appear as "CX987654321US". The tracking number provided by Shein allows customers to monitor the progress of their shipment and stay updated on the estimated delivery date. Customers can usually find the tracking number in their order confirmation email or on the Shein website by accessing their order history. By inputting the tracking number on the respective shipping carrier's website or using the tracking function on the Shein website, customers can easily track their package until it reaches its destination.
